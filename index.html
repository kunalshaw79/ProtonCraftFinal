Problem in Hydrogen Production

Grey Hydrogen

  • ~95% of global production — high CO₂ emissions
  • Steam methane reforming releases large CO₂ per kg H₂
  • Pricing and carbon costs make it unsustainable

Green Hydrogen — Cost & Water

  • Expensive due to high energy & purified water needs
  • Typical electrolysis uses freshwater; resource pressure exists
  • Need for more efficient, waste-driven feedstocks

Industrial HAZARD — Abundant Hydrogen Sulfide (H₂S)

  • Natural gas sources can contain up to ~28% H₂S — highly toxic.
  • Major H₂S sources: oil refineries, tanneries, coke ovens, gas wells.
  • Typical disposal is oxidation — low value and further emissions.

Our Solution

Transforming hydrogen sulfide into green hydrogen and industrial sulfur — on site, circular, and emission-free.
